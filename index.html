Interesting Places

•Parque del Retiro

It is filled with interesting sculptures and fountains such as the magnificent Mounument to alfonso, which watches over the lake.

•Palacio Real (royal palace)

The Royal Palace of Madrid is the official residence of the Spanish royal family at the city of Madrid, although now used only for state ceremonies.

•Museo Nacional del Prado The Prado has one of the largest art collections in the world, it is best known for its diverse art works.

 

Culture

The culture in Madrid is influenced by the cities long, historic role in Spanish and European history, its current role as thriving capital, its dominant position in the art scene, its incredible international food scene and its famous reputation for endless fiestas!

 

Food

•tortilla de patata

A thick potato omelette

•Paella

A yellow rice containing a variety of meats, (pork, chicken, rabbit, snail, squid), mussels, grated tomato and a variety of different vegetables and other random things.

•Churros y Porras

Spanish pastry fried in strips or curls, sweet and crunchy on the outside while soft on the inside. The main difference between churros (on the right) and porras (left) is that porras are thicker so more spongy or crunchy. These are usually eaten with thick hot chocolate

•Patatas bravas

Also known as papas bravas, they are thick potato cubes, fried and spicy with brava sauce. This are usually a sort of snack or side dish, often served at bars with a drink.

 

Sport

•stadium

Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a retractable roof football stadium in Madrid, Spain. With a seating capacity of 78,297 the stadium has the second-largest seating capacity for a football stadium in Spain. It has been the home stadium of Real Madrid since its completion in 1947

•football team

The top football club in Madrid is Real Madrid, is a Spanish professional football club based in Madrid. The club competes in La Liga, the top tier of Spanish football.

History

Madrid is a European city that was born within an Arab fortress, that was built by Emir Muhammad I of Córdoba next to the river Manzanares. The community was originally named ‘Mayrit’, alluding to its numerous streams and groundwater The Christians of northern Spain continued conquering Al Ándalus, and then settled in Madrid.
